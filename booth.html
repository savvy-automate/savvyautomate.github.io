Women in AI Event · Booth Playbook

Show up savvy.
Stand out smart.

Everything Wendy and Bridgette need to run a brilliant, on-brand booth — without blowing the budget.

01 — Visual presence

Display & signage

🪧

Pull-up banner

  • Logo + tagline in white on deep purple
  • Robot mascot large, centred — friendly and eye-catching
  • Website URL at the base in teal
  • Portrait format, positioned behind the table
🖥️

Table setup

  • Table runner — deep purple, white Savvy logo across front
  • A5 tent cards — "What we do" + QR to book a call
  • Laptop open — short looping Loom or Canva slides as live demo
  • Keep it uncluttered — white space is your friend

02 — Team look

What Wendy & Bridgette wear

👕

Matching branded tees

  • Black tee — white Savvy logo on the front, left chest
  • Quirky line on the back — see below
  • Comfortable, looks intentional without being stuffy
Back of tee: "I automate the boring stuff. You're welcome."
🪪

Accessories

  • Lanyards — purple with white logo, holds name badge
  • Optional cap — black with embroidered purple logo if budget allows
  • Optional tote — useful giveaway or carry bag for the day

03 — Things people actually keep

Low-cost giveaways

🤖

Robot mascot stickers

  • Die-cut sticker of the robot mascot — 50–100 units
  • Cheapest item at any event, most likely to be kept
  • Goes on laptops, notebooks, water bottles
  • Walking Savvy advertising long after the event
✏️

Branded stationery

  • Purple pens — "Savvy Automate — stop doing it manually" on the side
  • Mini notepads — purple cover, white logo. People use these at events.
  • Order 30 of each — enough without over-spending
📋

Free Automation Audit card

  • A6 printed card — "Book your free 30-min Automation Audit"
  • QR code linking to a Calendly or Tally booking form
  • Zero production cost beyond print — highest ROI item on the table
  • Positions you as experts, not sellers

04 — Drawing people in

Engagement & lead capture

🫙

"What's eating your time?" jar

  • People write their biggest manual task on a card, drop it in
  • Conversation starter that feels personal, not salesy
  • Read them out during the day — creates buzz at the booth
  • Post the best ones to LinkedIn after — free content
⚡

Live 2-min demo

  • Show one real n8n or Zapier workflow running live
  • Pick something visual — email automation, Slack notification, etc.
  • Nothing converts better than "watch this happen in real time"
  • Keep it under 2 minutes — people have short attention spans at events
📱

QR lead capture

  • Tally form — name, email, biggest automation pain point
  • Offer a free resource on submit (checklist, workflow template)
  • QR code printed large on a tent card on the table
  • Collects leads without awkward "can I have your card?" moments

05 — Copy ideas

Tagline options for the banner

"We talk to robots so you don't have to." "Your business, on autopilot." "Less admin. More everything else." "Stop doing it manually." "The no-code automation people."
Tent card header idea: "Drowning in manual tasks? We build automations that fix that." — short, direct, speaks to the exact pain.

Don't underestimate the mascot

That little robot is pure gold. Use it big on the banner, die-cut it as a sticker, and print it on the tee back. At an AI event, a friendly robot mascot is a genuine crowd-puller — it signals approachable expertise, not intimidating tech.
